vMOF

The Virtual Meeting Outcome Facilitator (vMOF) was a project that I worked on during my senior year with a team of students who were also preparing for graduation. We developed this in conjunction with The MITRE Corporation. Though a grueling process we eventually completed the project and were quite happy with how it came out. You can view our final presentation about the project, along with a live demo, here.

  • Client: The MITRE Corporation
  • Date: August 2014 - May 2015
  • Platform: Web/Mobile Browsers

Controller Painting

I decided a while back that I wanted to do a custom controller for when I play Super Smash Bros. One day I sat down and figured out everything I needed to get to make it happen, including the process to do it. While it didn't come out perfectly, I did learn a few lessons from it. When I do one again in the future I will take those lessons into it. This will hopefully allow me to make an even better end product.

  • Date: November 2014

Bus Stop Application

A prototype that a team and I had created for our usability class. In an effort to better understand what users looked for when using an unfamiliar application. By performing hallway tests to ensure that we were not hitting any major problems during our prototype we were able to count it as a success. Unfortunately never made it past the prototype stage, because an application that performed the exact same actions was released by the school mere months later.

  • Client: RIT Software Engineering Department
  • Date: October - November 2012
  • Platform: Android OS

Stream Overlay

After noticing the lack of a good stream overlay for my local Smash Bros Community I took it upon myself to create one. While having to learn how to not only create overlays, but also work with photoshop, as I had not in a while, the project was finished. While I still look at it and notice improvements I can make, the experience of making it and working with a customer who had differing ideas of what would look good on it was ultimatly a very rewarding experience.

  • Client: Smash the Roc/Rochester Smash Community
  • Date: September - 2015
  • Platform: Twitch
