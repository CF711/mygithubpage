vMOF

The Virtual Meeting Outcome Facilitator (vMOF) was a project that I worked on during my senior year with a team of students who were also preparing for graduation. We developed this in conjunction with The MITRE Corporation. The intention was to make a system that could act not only as a mediator, should conflict arise, but also as the name suggests a facilitator. The activities that we created for this system aimed to accomplish those goals, whether it was helping people come up with new ideas, helping users get inside the head of the customer through an interactive empathy map, or simply helping the team decide on a direction when they were unsure of what to do next. Though it was a grueling process we eventually completed the project and were quite happy with how it came out. You can view our final presentation about the project, along with a live demo, here.

  • Client: The MITRE Corporation
  • Date: August 2014 - May 2015
  • Platform: Web/Mobile Browsers

Controller Painting

Being involved in the competitive Super Smash Bros. community, I wanted something that would help me stand out when I played. I began doing research as to how to achieve the look that I wanted for my controller and what process was involved in creating such a thing. The end result wasn't perfect. There were portions where the paint was too thick from too many layers, where paint eventually rubbed off (from missing a portion of the controller with sealer), and where paint bled over to portions that were not intended to be painted/painted that color. These lessons and others will allow me to make a better version down the road.

  • Date: November 2014

Bus Stop Application

This was a prototype that a team and I had created for our usability class. In an effort to better understand how users interacted with an unfamiliar application and what mistakes we might be making as developers that could lead the users to not understand our applications. By performing hallway tests to ensure that we were not hitting any major problems with our major use cases we were able to count it as a success. Unfortunately never made it past the prototype stage, because an application that covered a similar user base was released by the school mere months later.

  • Client: RIT Software Engineering Department
  • Date: October - November 2012
  • Platform: Android OS

Stream Overlay

In the Rochester Super Smash Bros Community there were plenty of tournaments, but they had the problem of looking amateur. I made an attempt to rectify that problem. I had to not only learn how overlays work, but also how to build one in photoshop. I kept in contact with the team out there the entire time and took their advice along with taking creative liberties of my own. I am ultimately happy with how it came out. If I was to do this again I would make a full set that they could switch between for ads and the different screens, but at the time I did not have much time to work with and could only get one screen done.

  • Client: Smash the Roc/Rochester Smash Community
  • Date: September - 2015
  • Platform: Twitch
